THE story

PAST,PRESENT,FUTURE...

Years ago living under the shadows, a group of shady individuals represented what we would call in our modern days as freedom of will. Created to preserve people's rights and to stop the leaders of various races from their dictatorial ways, the order of the Skvll was what set the foundations and beliefs of what the now called Skvllpvnkz represent. Their ancestors fought for freedom, they fight for decentralization.

Years into the future, the 100 OG Skvllpvnkz have grown in numbers. Owning the streets of the block-chain, big regulatory corporations decided to take action and push them back. Using enforcing SkvllBots to fight the Skvllpvnkz, the bots forced the remaining of the Skvllpvnk resistance into underground bunkers called The Hideouts. Trying to survive they go out on regular scavenger hunts where they can find valuable loot and other collectibles.

So much to take in! We're sure you have a ton of questions so we tried to pull together some commonly asked ones. If you still have more questions then join our community on Discord.

First off you need an Ethereum wallet and some ETH. You can buy ETH from various exchanges like Coinbase & Binance. You then need a browser plugin called MetaMask that allows you to connect to the Ethereum blockchain and mint a Skvllpvnk as soon as we drop.

You can mint a maximum of 20 Skvllpvnkz per transaction. You can execute multiple transactions. We are doing this to keep gas prices low.

Is there a presale?

Where can I see my Skvllpvnkz?

All your minted Skvllpvnkz are in your wallet address that you used to buy. You can see them in your wallet (Metamask), Opensea collection or by clicking Gallery on the top right of the website.

Why buy a Skvllpvnk?

The first thing we suggest doing is joining our discord. That grants you access to everything that is going on around the project and its development. It is also where the Skvll Fam hangs out.

How are the Skvllpvnkz generated?

Using our own very special home-grown algorithm we precisely stitch together a selection of hand drawn layers such as masks, helmets, accessories and clothing. The algorithm is such that some combinations of properties, often called traits, will be more rare than others.
